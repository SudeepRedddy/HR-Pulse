1. LEAVE POLICY

All full-time employees are entitled to the following leave benefits per calendar year:

Annual Leave: Employees receive 20 paid vacation days per year, accrued monthly at 1.67 days per month. Unused leave may be carried forward up to a maximum of 5 days into the next calendar year. Any leave beyond the carryover limit will be forfeited on December 31. Employees must submit leave requests at least 5 business days in advance for planned absences and notify their direct manager within 2 hours for unplanned absences.

Sick Leave: Employees are granted 12 paid sick days per year. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days. Sick leave cannot be carried forward and resets on January 1 each year.

Parental Leave: Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers receive 4 weeks of fully paid leave. Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the birth or adoption of a child.

Bereavement Leave: Employees may take up to 5 paid days for the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling) and up to 3 paid days for extended family members.

Public Holidays: The company observes 10 national public holidays per year as published in the annual holiday calendar.

2. REMOTE WORK POLICY

HRPulse supports a hybrid work model to promote flexibility and work-life balance. Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week, subject to manager approval and role requirements. Fully remote arrangements may be granted on a case-by-case basis for roles that do not require physical presence.

Remote work expectations: Employees must be available during core business hours (10:00 AM to 4:00 PM local time) regardless of their work location. All remote employees must maintain a secure, reliable internet connection and a dedicated workspace free from distractions. Employees are expected to attend all scheduled meetings via video conferencing with cameras on unless otherwise agreed upon.

Equipment: The company provides a one-time home office stipend of $500 for remote-eligible employees to set up their workspace. IT equipment (laptop, monitor, peripherals) is provided by the company and must be returned upon separation.

Remote employees are required to visit the office at least once per month for team collaboration days, unless explicitly waived by their department head.

3. PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCESS

HRPulse conducts performance reviews on a quarterly basis to ensure continuous feedback and development. The review cycle operates as follows:

Q1 Review (January-March): Goal setting and alignment with company OKRs. Employees and managers collaboratively define 3-5 measurable goals for the quarter.

Q2 Review (April-June): Mid-year check-in. Progress review against Q1 goals, recalibration if needed, and preliminary feedback.

Q3 Review (July-September): Continued progress tracking with a focus on skill development and stretch assignments.

Q4 Review (October-December): Annual comprehensive review including self-assessment, peer feedback (360-degree), and manager evaluation. Compensation adjustments and promotion decisions are made based on the cumulative annual performance.

Rating Scale: Employees are rated on a scale of 1 to 4: (1) Needs Improvement — does not meet expectations, (2) Meets Expectations — satisfactory performance, (3) Exceeds Expectations — consistently delivers above requirements, (4) Outstanding — exceptional performance with significant impact.

Employees rated 1 for two consecutive quarters will be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) lasting 60 days. Failure to improve during the PIP period may result in termination.

4. CODE OF CONDUCT

All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional behavior. The following principles guide our workplace conduct:

Respect and Inclusion: We are committed to fostering an inclusive environment where every individual is treated with dignity and respect regardless of race, gender, age, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic. Discrimination, harassment, and bullying of any kind are strictly prohibited and will result in immediate disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Confidentiality: Employees must protect all proprietary and confidential company information. Sharing internal data, client information, or trade secrets with unauthorized parties is grounds for immediate termination and may result in legal action.

Conflict of Interest: Employees must disclose any outside business interests, investments, or relationships that may conflict with their responsibilities at HRPulse. All conflicts must be reported to the Ethics Committee within 5 business days of discovery.

Substance Policy: The use, possession, or distribution of illegal substances on company premises or during work hours is strictly prohibited. Moderate consumption of alcohol at company-sponsored events is permitted.

5. GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

HRPulse is committed to resolving employee grievances promptly and fairly. Employees who wish to raise a concern should follow these steps:

Step 1 — Informal Resolution: The employee should first attempt to resolve the issue directly with the individual involved or their immediate manager within 5 business days of the incident.

Step 2 — Formal Complaint: If the issue remains unresolved, the employee may file a formal written grievance with the HR department using the Grievance Form available on the HR portal. HR will acknowledge receipt within 2 business days.

Step 3 — Investigation: HR will conduct a thorough investigation within 10 business days. This may include interviews with relevant parties, review of documents, and consultation with legal counsel if necessary.

Step 4 — Resolution: HR will provide a written response to the complainant within 5 business days of completing the investigation, outlining findings and any corrective actions to be taken.

Step 5 — Appeal: If the employee is dissatisfied with the outcome, they may appeal to the Chief People Officer within 10 business days. The appeal decision is final and binding.

All grievances are treated confidentially. Retaliation against employees who file grievances in good faith is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action.
